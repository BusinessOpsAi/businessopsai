Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

This is a plain-English draft privacy policy for BusinessOps AI. It explains what information may be collected when you visit the website, request a pilot review, become an active pilot client, or use support.

Information collected through the Pilot Review Form

When you submit the Pilot Review Form, BusinessOps AI may collect your name, business name, email address, phone number, business type, current lead-response process, lead sources, workflow problems, preferred pilot scope, and any other details you choose to provide. This information is used to review fit, ask follow-up questions, and scope a possible managed pilot.

Information collected through Client Setup Intake

Approved clients may be asked to complete a private Client Setup Intake. That intake may collect business contact details, approved lead sources, service-area information, service categories, escalation rules, approved customer-facing language, tool access requirements, inbox preferences, and other setup details needed to configure a pilot. The Client Setup Intake is private and is not intended for general public use.

Information collected through the Support Request Form

Active pilot clients may use the Support Request Form to report workflow issues, request approved changes, or flag active lead-handling concerns. Support requests may include business contact details, issue descriptions, affected workflow areas, timestamps, screenshots or examples you choose to provide, and follow-up notes.

Lead and customer data processed for active clients

For active client workflows, BusinessOps AI may process lead or customer information needed to support the approved pilot scope. This may include names, contact details, service requests, location or service-area details, message history, status updates, review flags, owner summaries, and approved follow-up content. BusinessOps AI does not sell client customer lists.

Logs and operational records

BusinessOps AI may keep usage logs, support logs, and error logs to monitor workflow activity, diagnose issues, review usage, support clients, and improve pilot reliability. Logs are intended to support operations and troubleshooting, not to give AI unlimited control over customer decisions.

Third-party tools

BusinessOps AI may use third-party tools to operate the website, collect forms, store files, run approved workflows, send email, process AI requests, host pages, manage support, and handle payments when used. These tools may include Google Forms, Google Sheets, Google Drive, Make.com, OpenAI or other API providers, email providers, hosting providers, and payment providers. Information handled by those tools is also subject to their own policies and settings.

What not to submit through public forms

Do not submit passwords, API keys, payment information, private customer lists, account credentials, or other highly sensitive information through public forms. If sensitive access is needed for an approved client workflow, BusinessOps AI will coordinate an appropriate client-approved access method.

Client-owned inboxes

Client-owned inboxes may be connected only with approval. For live workflows, customer-facing replies should come from a client-approved business inbox when that is part of the approved scope. Clients may revoke tool or inbox access, but doing so may interrupt service.

How information is used

Information may be used to respond to questions, review pilot fit, scope and configure approved workflows, provide support, route review-needed leads, create owner summaries, monitor usage, troubleshoot errors, send service communications, and maintain records needed to operate the service.

Sharing

BusinessOps AI does not sell client customer lists. Information may be shared with service providers only as reasonably needed to operate the website, forms, approved workflows, support, hosting, communications, or payments. Information may also be shared if required by law or needed to protect legitimate business interests.

Data changes and removal

Clients and prospects can request corrections, changes, or removal of information by contacting BusinessOps AI. Requests will be handled subject to operational needs, support history, legal obligations, backup limitations, payment records, and other reasonable business requirements.

Contact

Questions about privacy can be sent to businessopsai@gmail.com.
