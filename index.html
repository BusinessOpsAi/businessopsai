Lead intake

Professional Lead Intake Options

BusinessOps AI does not require your customers to fill out a generic Google Form. For client-facing lead capture, a pilot can connect to approved intake paths such as an existing website form, a business lead inbox, a clean hosted intake page, an embedded form on the client's website, or a dedicated lead inbox such as leads@yourbusiness.com. The goal is to keep the customer experience professional while BusinessOps AI operates quietly in the background.

Email identity

Client-Owned Email Identity

For live client workflows, customer-facing replies should come from a client-approved business inbox, not from BusinessOps AI's admin email. A dedicated inbox such as leads@yourbusiness.com or estimates@yourbusiness.com is preferred. This keeps replies professional, keeps customer conversations with the business, and lets BusinessOps AI manage the workflow in the background.

Why leads slip

Most service businesses do not lose leads because they lack demand. They lose them in the handoff.

Calls arrive during jobs, forms sit too long, customer details live in different places, and follow-up depends on whoever remembers next. BusinessOps AI helps install structure around those moments.

Human review

Human Review Stays Built In

BusinessOps AI is not designed to give AI unlimited control. The workflow can help with safe first replies, collecting missing details, routing review-needed leads, one polite follow-up, owner summaries, usage logging, and support logging. Human review is required for exact quotes, final pricing, final scheduling, refunds, discounts, billing questions, complaints, angry customers, damage or injury claims, legal issues, emergencies, outside service areas, services not offered, and unclear or low-confidence requests.

FAQ

Questions a cautious owner should ask before starting.

Is this just a chatbot?

No. BusinessOps AI focuses on managed lead-response pilots around first replies, follow-up, human-review routing, owner summaries, and operational visibility. Chat can be one part of the system, but the value is in the workflow design and oversight.

What businesses is this for?

It is built for local service businesses, home service teams, field service teams, agencies, owner-operators, and small teams that miss leads or struggle with follow-up.

Will AI replace my team?

No. The pilot is designed to support your team by handling repetitive lead-response work, preparing information, and routing sensitive cases for review. Human judgment remains important for exact quote requests, services not offered, outside-service-area requests, refunds, discounts, billing questions, complaints, angry customers, damage, injury, legal issues, and low-confidence responses.

How does the pilot work?

BusinessOps AI starts with a pilot review, scopes workflow rules, recommends the pilot level, configures and tests agents, launches after approval, then monitors usage and support requests.

How long does setup take?

Setup timing depends on workflow scope, lead sources, tool access, and integration complexity. Simple pilots are designed to move quickly after discovery and access are complete.

Can this work with my current tools?

In many cases, yes. The pilot can be scoped around common tools such as email, forms, calendars, CRMs, spreadsheets, and task systems, depending on access and workflow requirements.

What does human approval mean?

Human approval means certain actions are drafted, routed, or flagged for a person to review before they are finalized, especially exact quote requests, services not offered, outside service area, refunds, discounts, billing questions, complaints, angry customers, damage, injury, legal issues, and low-confidence responses.

What happens if usage grows?

Plans include monthly usage allowances. Essential lead handling should not silently stop when usage increases; BusinessOps AI reviews usage with the client and can recommend overage handling or a plan adjustment.

Can you build a custom workflow?

Yes. Custom Ops work is scoped after review. Deeper integrations typically start around $1,500, but smaller custom add-ons may be scoped separately.

How do support requests work?

Support requests are available for active pilot clients. Low-priority questions are logged and acknowledged. Urgent workflow issues and active lead-handling problems are escalated internally.
